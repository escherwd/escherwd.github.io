Replacing your iPhone's housing

If you look closely at your phone, you'll notice that your phone is seperated into two parts; the face, with the screen and home button, and the housing, which wraps around the sides and back of the phone. Unbeknownst to most, you can order custom housings to make your phone unique.

via bgr.com

via bgr.com

To install one of these, the phone has to be gutted, then reinstalled to the new housing.

A common trend in iPhone housing designs is making phones look newer than they actually are. For example, an iPhone 6s housing that looks like the iPhone 7.

My own iPhone 6s

Former Space-Grey 6s


WHERE TO BUY THEM

Most of these parts ship straight from China, so it does take a while for them to arrive.

DHGate
eBay
TrueSupplier

Use a term similar to "iPhone <your model> housing" when searching on these websites.

INSTALLATION

Installing a new housing on an iPhone takes a couple of hours (~4 or so), thats why the sevice fee is so much higher than the others. It is important that you have the housing when you come to me, I'm not going to take the time to order it.

Privacy

During the replacement process, the phone will be completely powered down. The only time I will power up the phone is for testing the fix. I will never open up the phone beyond the lockscreen (adding a password is reccomended when sending iPhones in to get fixed).

Warranty / Promise

Please only send in phones if you know exactly what the problem is with them. If the screen is black, the phone doesn't vibrate, and nothing happens when you charge it, don't send it in asking for the screen to be fixed and expecting everything to be ok. Only if you know that the part you are trying to fix is the problem should you send it in.

I don't accept responsibility for problems that you didn't ask me to fix (i.e I fix the screen but the camera is still broken).

If I break a part that was completely functional when the phone was sent in, it will be fixed on the owner's behalf

If you find any issues I caused within 3 days of me returning the phone, then they will be fixed at no extra cost. Only if I caused the problem. (i.e if you smash your screen 2 days after I repair that's not my fault and I'm not fixing it)
